What to Look for in Any Hauler

Not all junk removal companies are equal. Here's how to evaluate any company before hiring.

  • Licensed and insured in New Jersey

    Always ask for proof of insurance. An uninsured crew working on your property creates real liability risk for you as the homeowner.

  • Free on-site estimates

    Reputable companies quote after seeing the job — not over the phone. Binding phone quotes based on your description almost always lead to surprise upcharges.

  • Donation-first policy

    Ask where your items go. A company that can name their donation partners and recycling facilities is one that has thought through its environmental obligations.

  • Verifiable local reviews

    Google reviews from local customers are the most trustworthy signal. Look for specific details about the job, not generic praise.

  • Transparent all-inclusive pricing

    The price quoted on-site should be the price you pay — including labor, hauling, and disposal. No add-ons at the end.

Red Flags to Watch For

  • ⚠Only provides phone quotes without seeing your items
  • ⚠Requires upfront payment before any work begins
  • ⚠No physical address, website, or online presence
  • ⚠Can't provide proof of insurance
  • ⚠Unusually low quote that grows significantly on arrival
  • ⚠Vague answer about where your materials go
