Garage Cleanout Guide for NJ Homeowners

Finally reclaim your garage — a practical guide for Warren County homeowners on sorting, donating, and hauling everything out.

The average American garage can hold two cars but is used for two cars' worth of stuff instead. If you're parking in the driveway while your garage holds decades of accumulated tools, furniture, holiday decorations, and mystery boxes — you're not alone. The good news is that most garages can be completely cleared in a single day with the right approach.

For Warren County homeowners, this guide gives you a realistic plan and connects you with Hackettstown Junk Removal — the area's most reliable local crew for garage cleanouts.

Before the Crew Arrives: Your Pre-Cleanout Checklist

  • Pull everything you're keeping to the driveway or inside the house
  • Remove gas cans (even partially full ones) — these can't go in a junk truck
  • Drain fuel from lawn mowers and power equipment
  • Remove propane tanks and cylinders
  • Disconnect and empty any mini-fridges or chest freezers 24 hours ahead
  • Set aside oil-based paint, pesticides, and motor oil for HHW disposal

💡 Pro tip

  • Old working tools often sell fast on Facebook Marketplace — list them the week before your cleanout
  • Metal scrap (old bikes, steel shelving, car parts) may be worth something at a local scrap yard
  • Tires can usually be taken by junk haulers for a small per-tire fee ($5–$15 each)

What Junk Removal Companies Take From Garages

Standard items: Old furniture stored in garage, broken appliances, bikes and sporting equipment, cardboard boxes, tools and hardware, shelving units, tires (small upcharge), yard equipment, paint cans (latex only, must be dried), bagged garbage, general clutter.

Requires special handling: Gas cans with fuel, oil-based paint and solvents, propane tanks, automotive fluids, fluorescent shop lights. These go through Warren County's HHW program instead.

How long does a garage cleanout take?

A single-car garage takes 1–2 hours with a 2-person crew. A packed two-car garage may take 3–4 hours. Most jobs are done in a single visit.

Do I need to carry things out myself?

No — the crew does all the loading. You just indicate what's going and what's staying.
