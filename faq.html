Is junk removal worth it vs. renting a dumpster?

For most NJ residential cleanouts, professional junk removal is more cost-effective once you factor in the dumpster delivery fee ($150–$250), the rental period, your own labor loading it, and final pickup and disposal. A junk crew does all the loading. See our full dumpster vs. junk removal comparison.

What factors affect junk removal pricing?

Main drivers: volume (truck fraction), weight (heavy materials cost more to dispose), labor difficulty (stairs, narrow spaces), material type (electronics and appliances may have fees), and timing (same-day service may carry a small premium).

What Gets Removed

What will junk removal companies take in NJ?

Furniture, mattresses, appliances, electronics, clothing, boxes, yard waste, light construction debris, bikes, sporting equipment, shelving, and general household clutter.

What items CAN'T go in a junk removal truck in NJ?

Oil-based paint and solvents, propane tanks with fuel, fluorescent bulbs, car batteries, pesticides, herbicides, motor oil, and other hazardous materials. These go through Warren County's HHW program or certified disposal facilities.

Do junk removal companies take electronics in NJ?

Yes, most will — but many charge a small fee for TVs, monitors, and computers because NJ requires electronics to be processed through certified e-waste recyclers. Ask upfront about electronics fees.

What happens to my junk after it's removed?

Responsible haulers follow a disposal hierarchy: donation first for usable items, recycling for metals and electronics, then licensed landfill disposal for what remains. Up to 60–70% of a typical load can be diverted from landfill.
